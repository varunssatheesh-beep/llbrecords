Moot Court

Reports for moots participated and observed

Part 1 of 3
📌
Total moots to cover: 2 Detailed Participated Moots + 1 Judgement Moot + ~17–18 Observed Moots using FIRAC (excluding Rules). Each observed moot = 4–6 pages.

📊 Moot Report Types & Requirements

Moot Type of Report Length Format
9th Semester Moot Participated ~8 pages Detailed full report
10th Semester Moot Participated ~8 pages Detailed full report
Latest Judged Moot Judged ~8 pages Written as a Judgement
17–18 Observed Moots Observed 4–6 pages each FIRAC method (no Rules section) + Arguments of both sides
📄 Detailed Moot Report (8 pages)

9th & 10th Semester Moots

Write a full detailed report of each moot you participated in. Include the problem, your arguments, counter-arguments, questions from judges, and outcome. ~8 pages each.

✍️ 2 reports × 8 pages = ~16 pages
⚖️ Judgement Format (8 pages)

Latest Judged Moot

Write this moot as a Judgement — with facts, issues raised, arguments from both petitioner and respondent, and the final decision/ruling.

⚖️ 1 judgement = ~8 pages
🔍 FIRAC Method (4–6 pages)

17–18 Observed Moots

Use FIRAC format — Facts, Issues, Arguments (both sides), Conclusion. Skip the Rules section. Include arguments of Petitioner and Respondent clearly.

📝 ~18 moots × 5 pages = ~90 pages
💡
FIRAC for Observed Moots: Facts → Issues → Arguments (Petitioner side + Respondent side) → Conclusion. Skip the "Rules" section. Each moot should cover 4–6 pages.

Court Work

Day-wise observation records + 2 full trial reports

Part 2 of 3
📌
Total: 15 days of court observation (each day = up to 4 pages) + 2 full trial reports (1 Civil + 1 Criminal).

📅 Day-wise Format — What to Write Each Day

  • Name of the Court — e.g., District Court, Family Court, Magistrate Court, etc.
  • Number of Cases Observed — total count of cases heard that day.
  • Name of the Presiding Officer — Judge / Magistrate's name and designation.
  • Court's Jurisdiction — territorial and subject matter jurisdiction of that court.
  • 3 Main Cases Elaborated — pick 3 cases and describe the procedures observed in detail (parties, nature of case, hearing stage, what happened).
📆 Day-wise Observation

15 Days × 4 Pages Each

For each of your 15 visit days, follow the fixed format above. Elaborate on 3 key cases per day with procedural details.

📄 Total: ~60 pages for day-wise section
⚖️ Civil Trial — Full Report

One Full Civil Trial

Write a complete report of one civil case you observed — from filing stage to the point it had reached on your date of visit. Include all procedures, hearings, documents filed, and orders passed.

📝 Up to date of your visit
🔒 Criminal Trial — Full Report

One Full Criminal Trial

Write a complete report of one criminal case observed — from FIR / complaint stage to the point it had reached on your date of visit.

📝 Up to date of your visit
⚠️
Important: The 2 full trial reports (Civil + Criminal) are separate from the day-wise entries. They should cover the full timeline of the case up to the date you visited.

Client Interview & Pre-Trial Preparation

Interviews + civil and criminal documents

Part 3 of 3
📌
Start with: 1 Civil Client Interview + 1 Criminal Client Interview. Then write all documents listed below in order. Collect originals from an advocate's office and recreate them in the record keeping the same format, but shorten facts to fit the ~60-page limit.
👤 Step 1 — Interview

Civil Client Interview

Write the full client interview in a structured Q&A or narrative format. Include client's background, nature of dispute, facts, reliefs sought, and advice given.
